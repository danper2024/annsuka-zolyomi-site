Annuska Zolyomi

I am a researcher and educator focused on inclusive technology design, disability studies, and human-computer interaction. My work explores how marginalized communities engage with digital tools and how we can build systems that reflect their lived experiences.

This site shares my research, teaching, and lab activities. I believe in designing with—not just for—disabled communities, and I strive to center lived experience in both scholarship and practice.

Research

My research centers on accessibility, assistive technologies, and participatory design. I collaborate with disabled communities to co-create tools that empower and reflect their needs.

  • Designing inclusive interfaces for neurodivergent users
  • Exploring storytelling as a method for accessibility research
  • Publications in CHI, ASSETS, and CSCW

Teaching

I teach courses on human-centered design, disability studies, and research methods. My pedagogy emphasizes critical thinking, community engagement, and reflective practice.

  • DIS ST 230: Disability and Technology
  • HCDE 518: Inclusive Design Studio
  • Guest lectures in HCI and STS programs

Lab

The Inclusive Tech Lab is a space for collaborative research, design, and advocacy. We work with community partners to prototype accessible tools and challenge dominant narratives in tech.

  • Current members: UX designers, disability activists, HCI researchers
  • Projects: Accessible journaling apps, sensory-friendly web design
  • Open to new collaborators and student researchers
