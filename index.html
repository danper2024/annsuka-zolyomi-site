Annuska Zolyomi

Educator, UX Researcher, Accessibility Specialist

My Experience

I am an Assistant Professor at the University of Washington Bothell. I hold a PhD in Information Science and a Master’s in Human-Centered Design and Engineering. My industry experience includes roles at Microsoft, Amazon, and Sesame Workshop as a program manager, product planner, and accessibility strategist.

Since 2013, I’ve consulted on UX and accessibility projects for clients including Microsoft, Amazon, Easter Seals, and the Joan Ganz Cooney Center at Sesame Workshop.

My Skillset

  • UX Research & Design
  • Accessibility Strategy
  • Participatory Design
  • Affective Computing
  • Program Management
  • Inclusive Technology Development

My Goals

My work centers on inclusive design and accessibility for neurodiverse communities. I aim to amplify autistic voices through participatory research and storytelling. I co-produced the Autism Stories podcast and have been quoted in Gizmodo on sarcasm detection and autism discourse.

I strive to build technologies that support emotional expression and social connection for autistic adults.
